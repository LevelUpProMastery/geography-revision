Green Revolution

Key Point: The Green Revolution used high-yielding seeds, fertilisers and irrigation to increase food supply.

  • Started in the 1960s due to population growth
  • HYVs (high-yielding varieties) like IR-8 doubled rice yields
  • Required fertilisers and controlled irrigation
  • Reduced growing period from 180 to 100 days

Benefits

  • Increased food production
  • Farmers earned more income
  • Improved living standards

Disadvantages

  • Expensive for poorer farmers
  • Environmental damage (pollution, soil erosion)
  • Waterlogging and salinisation
  • Increased inequality

CAP (Common Agricultural Policy)

  • Protect farmer incomes
  • Ensure stable food supply
  • Keep prices stable
  • Protect rural life

Problems: Overproduction, expensive food, environmental damage.

Farm Diversification

Farmers earn extra income by:

  • Farm shops and PYO (pick your own)
  • Holiday cottages
  • Golf courses and nature reserves

Types of Farming

  • Arable: crops
  • Pastoral: animals
  • Mixed: both
  • Subsistence: for own use
  • Commercial: for profit
  • Intensive: high inputs
  • Extensive: low inputs

Factors Affecting Farming

Human factors: labour, capital, technology, markets, government

Physical factors: climate, soil, relief

Farming System

  • Inputs: labour, money, soil, fertiliser
  • Processes: planting, harvesting
  • Outputs: crops, milk, meat
